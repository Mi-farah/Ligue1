# Ligue 1 Carbon Footprint Analysis & Visualization Project

## Project Overview
I am building a comprehensive web application to analyze and visualize the carbon emissions generated by travel in French Ligue 1 football. The project aims to highlight the environmental impact of different transportation methods used by teams and fans traveling between stadiums across France.

## Problem Statement
French Ligue 1 football teams and their supporters travel extensively throughout the season, generating significant carbon emissions. Currently, there's limited visibility into:
- The environmental impact of different travel modes (planes, trains, buses, cars)
- Comparative analysis of emissions between transportation methods
- The potential for reducing emissions through policy changes or travel restrictions

## Key Features Required

### 1. Interactive Comparison Dashboard
- Side-by-side comparison of emissions for each match route
- Visual representation showing the dramatic difference between air travel (27,000+ kg CO2) vs train travel (200-2,000 kg CO2)
- Interactive filters by team, route, or transportation method

### 2. Team-Specific Analysis
- Total emissions per team for the entire season
- Ranking of teams by environmental impact
- Breakdown by home vs away travel patterns

### 3. Policy Impact Simulation
- Interactive tool to simulate travel restrictions (e.g., "No flights under 3 hours")
- Automatic routing to alternative transportation when restrictions apply
- Real-time calculation of emission reductions

### 4. Geographic Visualization
- Map showing all Ligue 1 stadiums and their locations
- Route visualization between stadiums
- Color-coded emissions overlay on map
